German Pronunciation Rules

Date: 08-01-2026

Introduction

This document covers key German pronunciation rules with examples.

Examples:

  • kuchen (কুখেন) - cake
  • küche (ক্যুখে) - kitchen

Rule 1: Pronunciation of "ch"

The pronunciation of "ch" depends on the preceding vowel:

a) After front vowels (a, i, e, ei, ä, ö)

  • Pronounced like স/শ (soft "sh" sound)
  • Examples:
    • küche (ক্যুখে) - kitchen
    • ich (ইখ) - I
    • sprechen (স্প্রেখেন) - to speak

b) After back vowels (a, o, u, au)

  • Pronounced like খ (hard "kh" sound)
  • Examples:
    • Bach (বাখ) - stream
    • Buch (বুখ) - book
    • auch (আউখ) - also

Rule 2: Pronunciation of "ig" at word end

When "ig" appears at the end of a word, it is pronounced like ইস ("is").

Example:

  • Honig → হনিস (honey)

Rule 3: Pronunciation of "ei"

The combination "ei" is pronounced like আই ("ai").

Examples:

  • nein → নাইন (no)
  • dein → ডাইন (your)
  • kein → কাইন (no/not a)

Rule 4: Pronunciation of "ie"

The combination "ie" is pronounced like ই ("i").

Examples:

  • schreiben → শ্রাইবেন (to write)
  • schrie → শ্রী (cried)

Rule 5: Pronunciation of "er" at word end

When "er" appears at the end of a word, it is pronounced like আ ("a").

Examples:

  • Vater → ফাটার (father)
  • Mutter → মুটার (mother)

Rule 6: Pronunciation of "eu" and "äu"

Both "eu" and "äu" are pronounced like অয় ("oy").

Examples:

  • Euro → ইউরো (Euro)
  • Bäu → বয় (bau)
  • Europa → ইউরোপা (Europe)
  • Bau → বাউ (construction)
